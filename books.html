  • Blockade Billy

    • Stephen King

    8/10. A quick read (1-2hr) by Stephen King that I couldn't put down. Baseball fans can't go wrong with this one.

  • Reality Is Not What It Seems

    • Carlo Rovelli

    7/10. An introduction to quantum physics (no math background required) that I found really interesting. In my opinion, Rovelli focuses too much on the developmental history of the topic rather than the actual semantics of quantum physics. Nonetheless, was cool reading about the progression of thought from Pythagoras and Newton to Bohr, Einstein, and Feynman.

  • A Random Walk Down Wall Street

    • Burton Malkiel

    9/10. Fun introduction to many basic must-know concepts of the finance world. Utilizes a bunch of cool historical events from the street to explain abstract topics. Issued as part of many finance classes in university.

  • Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience

    • Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi

    9/10. Read this. Seriously. Even if you think you have it all figured out, I'm sure you can learn something from Csikszentmihalyi's research. If you love sports/fitness/chess/etc or have an immense passion for something, this book helps make sense of the particular feeling a passion exudes and how you can attempt to replicate it.

Daniel Berlin, 2019
